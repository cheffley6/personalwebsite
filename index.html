February 2021

Rock Paper Scissors SVM

As part of my junior design course in the Robotic Musicianship Lab, another student and I were tasked with building a system that can use supervised learning to watch a game of rock paper scissors via ultrasound imagery and determine the winner. To do this, we attached ultrasound probes to our forearms and trained two copies of a supervised learning model using Max MSP and SKLearn's support vector classifier. We then counted down a timer sent between two Windows machines using UDP, and we played rock paper scissors, where a live image would be sent from each probes to separate systems, which then predicted whether a hand was holding rock, paper, or scissors; then, each system shared its prediction with the other to determine a winner of the game.
